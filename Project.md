## Project Description

This project, led by Shape Policy and delivered in partnership with Australia Policy Online (APO), explores how an online policy repository can be responsibly made ready for the “AI Era”. Students will work with Shape Policy and the APO to design and build an open-source plugin for the Drupal content management system that enables AI systems to access a policy repository database through a structured interface, such as the model context protocol (MCP). Shape Policy is the lead organisation on the project, facilitating collaboration with the APO and supporting the student team. Shape Policy brings experience working on projects at the intersection of policy and AI and see’s the development of open-source AI and its use in policy research and analysis as its core mission.

APO is Australia’s leading open-access repository of public policy research, reports, and grey literature. For over two decades, APO has played a critical role in supporting evidence-based policy by aggregating, curating, and publishing policy-relevant materials from government, academia, think tanks, and the non-profit sector. Its audience includes staff across all levels of government, researchers, journalists, students, and civil society organisations. The APO currently has more than 45,000 policy resources, APO's website receives more than 400,000 annual visits from AU/NZ, with over 14,600 subscribers (working across government, research and advocacy organisations) to its free newsletter service.

At a time when policy problems are becoming more complex and time-pressured, access to high-quality, trusted policy data is increasingly important. Yet the way policy professionals discover, navigate, and synthesise information is changing rapidly and AI systems are becoming part of everyday knowledge work.

APO sits at the centre of this emerging landscape. Its content is trusted, carefully curated, and governed by clear metadata and access rules. However, like many public-interest repositories, it was designed primarily for human users navigating via a web interface, not for AI systems acting as automated research assistants. This project will explore how a public policy repository can be responsibly adapted for an AI-enabled future.

## Project Aim

AI systems are increasingly being used to answer policy questions, summarise evidence, and support decision-making. Yet most AI tools operate by scraping the open web or relying on training data, making it difficult to ensure that answers are grounded in authoritative sources or that original publishers are credited or cited correctly.

At the same time, repositories like APO already hold structured, high-quality policy data, supported by rich metadata describing topics, jurisdictions, publication types, authorship, and access conditions. The challenge is not a lack of data, but a lack of infrastructure that allows AI systems to interact with that data appropriately.

This project seeks to address the challenge of making high quality policy data available to AI systems. The aim of this project is to design and build an open-source software module for Drupal that enables AI systems to access policy data managed using the CMS through a standardised interface. The module will need to expose a repository to AI systems in a structured way (such as via MCP), support semantic (meaning-based) retrieval across large collections (such as via retrieval augmented generation), and allow for different tiers of access and use aligned with existing access and licensing frameworks such as Creative Commons. For students, the project offers an opportunity to work on a real, meaningful system at the intersection of software engineering, data, AI, policy and governance.

## Desired Student Skills

This project is suited to students with interests across software engineering, data systems, and applied AI. Students undertaking the project should be comfortable working with web technologies and backend systems, and willing to learn new frameworks and standards as needed.

Relevant skills and interests include experience with content management systems (such as Drupal or similar platforms), MCP/API design and integration, data modelling, and familiarity with AI or information retrieval concepts. Experience with Python, JavaScript, or PHP will be useful, as will an interest in semantic search, metadata, or large-scale data systems. Just as important are problem-solving skills, the ability to work collaboratively in a team, and an interest in building systems that operate in real organisational and governance contexts. The project does not require prior expertise in all of these areas; students with complementary skill sets are encouraged to apply and learn together.

## Outcomes and Opportunities

Students who choose this project will gain experience working on a real-world digital infrastructure challenge with a national policy organisation. They will learn how large, trusted data repositories are structured and governed, and how those structures can be adapted to support emerging AI use cases.

Through the project, students will develop practical skills in system architecture, data access and retrieval, and AI integration, while also engaging with broader questions about trust, transparency, and sustainability in AI systems. They will have the opportunity to contribute to an open-source software project with potential impact beyond the university, and to see how technical decisions interact with policy, licensing, and organisational needs.

For students interested in careers in government, public policy, technology consulting, data engineering, or responsible AI, this project offers a rare opportunity to work at the intersection of these domains on a problem of genuine public value.
